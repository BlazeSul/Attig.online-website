Attiq Consumer Data Right (CDR) Policy

Last updated: 23 July 2026

Version 1.0

This CDR Policy explains how Attiq ("the app", "we", "us") handles Consumer Data Right (CDR) data when you choose to connect a bank account. It applies only if and when you use the optional bank-connection feature. It sits alongside our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service. For all other data (data you type, scan, or import), the Privacy Policy governs.

Bank connection is an optional feature. If you never connect a bank, no CDR data is ever collected and this policy does not apply to you.

1. About the Consumer Data Right

The Consumer Data Right is an Australian law that lets you securely share data held about you by your bank with accredited businesses you choose. In banking, the CDR is often called "Open Banking". Sharing is always your choice, is time-limited, and can be withdrawn by you at any time.

2. Our role and our data recipient

Attiq is not itself an Accredited Data Recipient (ADR). Bank connections are provided through Basiq, which is accredited under the CDR. When you connect a bank, you authorise the collection of your banking data directly with Basiq and your bank through the official CDR consent flow. Attiq accesses that data as a provider working with Basiq, so that we can show it to you inside the app. Your bank login credentials are never entered into, seen by, or stored by Attiq.

3. What CDR data we handle

With your consent, the following categories of CDR data may be collected to power the features you asked for:

  • account details (account name, type, and masked account number);
  • account balances;
  • transaction data (date, amount, description, and merchant where available).

We collect only the data needed to display your accounts and transactions and to categorise your spending inside Attiq. We do not collect your name, date of birth, or contact details through the CDR for marketing or any unrelated purpose.

4. How we use CDR data

CDR data is used to:

  • show your connected accounts, balances, and transactions in the app;
  • automatically categorise transactions and generate your spending insights, budgets, and charts;
  • keep those views up to date while your consent is active.

We do not sell CDR data, use it for advertising, or share it with third parties for their own purposes. CDR data is not used to make automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about you.

5. Consent — and how to withdraw it

You control your consent. When you connect a bank you will see, through the CDR flow, exactly what data is shared, for what purpose, and for how long. You can:

  • withdraw your consent at any time from within Attiq (disconnect the bank connection), which stops further collection;
  • manage or withdraw consent directly with Basiq or with your bank;
  • choose to have your CDR data deleted rather than de-identified when your consent ends (see below).

6. Storage, security, and de-identification

CDR data displayed in the app is held on your device. Where CDR data is processed on our servers to deliver the connection, it is protected in transit and at rest with encryption and access controls. When your consent expires or you withdraw it, CDR data that is no longer required is deleted or de-identified in accordance with the CDR rules and Basiq's accredited processes.

7. Your CDR rights

Under the CDR you have the right to:

  • choose what data you share and for how long;
  • see and manage your active data-sharing arrangements;
  • withdraw consent at any time;
  • request that your CDR data be deleted;
  • make a complaint (see below) and escalate it externally.

8. Complaints

If you have a concern about how your CDR data has been handled, contact us first at feedback@attiq.online and we will acknowledge and work to resolve it. If you are not satisfied, you can escalate to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au, which handles CDR privacy complaints.

9. Changes to this policy

We will update this policy as the bank-connection feature and the CDR rules evolve, and revise the version and date above. Material changes will be reflected here before they take effect.
